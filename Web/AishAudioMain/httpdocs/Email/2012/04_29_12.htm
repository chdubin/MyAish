I Owe It to Myself to Grow

 KY 142 D
by Kirzner, Rabbi Yitzchak zt"l

What is the attraction for wanting to grow? We know that Aaron, the prototype of one who 'loved and chased peace', was able to heal deep rifts between people. Now hear how he kindled a thirst for learning Torah and wanting to personally grow.

Advanced

  
 

Feeling As a Beloved Child

KV 843
by Kalmanovitz, Rebbitzen V.

As Jews, we are especially close to Hashem, and to further enhance our lives He gave us the Torah. Rebbitzen Kalmanovits describes the immense love God has for us and His ever flowing kindness. So what is obstructing our feeling the full warmth of this connection, and how we can begin to remove the barriers?

Advanced

  
 

Shelanu: 4 Ways Hashem Answers My Prayers

NB 678
by Nissel, Rabbi Menachem

We've often heard people say "I asked, but God said no". While this may be an admirable response to being denied what we desperately wanted, were these heartfelt prayers simply cast to the wayside? Rabbi Nissel proves that tefillah works. And even when we think it didn't, he shows how answers are often disguised in ways we wouldn't recognize unless we learn how to look for them.

Advanced

  
 

Iyar: Pesach Sheni & Lag B'Omer

 ON 528 B
by Orlowek, Rabbi Noach

Class A: Before understanding how the month of Iyar sets the stage for accepting the Torah, Rabbi Orlowek asks us consider a few Kabbalistic sources. A profound class on how your emotional capacity is intrinsically connected to your deeper potential for learning Torah.

Class B: The month of Iyar was created with the sixth letter letter of the Alef-Beis,'Vav'. The continuity and stability of this letter connects us to the essence of Iyar and to the inner work we need to do. This esoteric class shows why Iyar is the bridge between Pesach and Shavuos, the period that underscores our unique individuality.

Destiny Part Three: Why the Jews?

 KE 793 C
by Spiro, Rabbi Ken

What sense does it make that Jews were hated from the moment they became a nation? Would knowing what caused such virulent anti-Semitism help to control it? Theories abound as to what the causes are, including: economic power, Zionism, elitism, being the scapegoat and several more. This class disproves them all one by one - leaving a surprising conclusion as the bottom line.
