Weekly Torah Portion: Naso

New Releases

Advanced

  
 

Humility - The Jewish Perspective

 BY 607
by Berkovits, Rabbi Yitzchak

Many feel that humility is an emotionally damaging character trait - where the more humility you have, the less you think of yourself. But isn't self-esteem a basic need in this rough and tumble world? Rav Berkovits' mature expose of this middah shows us how living without an inflated ego can free us from constantly comparing and jealousy, and can make us happier and more at peace with the world.

Introductory

  
 

Sabbath: A Weekly Appraisal

 GA 712
by Geller, Rabbi Avi

The Sabbath was a special gift to the Jewish people. A supernatural day to step back from our hectic lives, take a deep breath, and refocus on who we are and how we'd like to live. Hear a lively content filled overview of the day - as only Rabbi Geller can deliver.

Introductory

  
 

The Jewish Meaning of Love

 WG 611 B
by Wagensberg, Rabbi Abba

The concepts of love and caring have been so bandied about - without really defining what they mean. Follow Rabbi Wagensberg's fascinating explanation starting from our pre-emptive Egyptian exit to an astounding story of a young girl suffering from paralysis, who hears the secret of bearing the pain of others - revealing to us the unity we can feel being truly bound to another.

Intro/Adv

  
 

How Meaning Relieves Suffering

 KY 912 A
by Kirzner, Rabbi Yitzchak zt"l

For every sincere understanding it is important to sort out the emotions from the intellect, and to own up to how much one affects the other. Rabbi Kirzner gives basic rules of psychology culled from Torah sources to help us understand not only the questions we ask, but why we're asking them.

Advanced

  
 

Your Best Environment

HT 789 I
by Heller, Rebbetzin Tziporah

How possible is it to define what our ideal environment is seeing that we are all so different? After all, some are affected by the positive role models in their lives, while others thrive on adversity, and others by confronting the negative influences in their lives. Rebbetzin Heller helps to define which surroundings help us to grow, and how to deal with different groupings of people that are common in many communities.
