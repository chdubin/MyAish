Weekly Torah Portion: Korach

New Releases

Advanced

  
 

Traits of a Leader

 ON 687
by Orlowek, Rabbi Noach

Moshe was a quintessential leader. By analyzing his character we can deduce the essential traits for leadership, whether it be over a community, a classroom, our children, or ourselves. Also hear about believing in the potential of those you are leading, and how a leader should confront those who have made mistakes?

Introductory

  
 

The Beauty of Shabbos - The Maharal

 WD 510 C
by Weinberg, Rebbetzin Denah

The Maharal was a noted thinker of his day and a prolific writer of esoteric thought on crucial Jewish issues. Rebbetzin Weinberg presents ideas from his thesis on Shabbos which include: what it means that the Divine has finished a task, the essence of the numbers six and seven, and why Shabbos is called 'The Bride'.

Advanced

  
 

Creating Yourself to Live Forever

 ZY 614
by Zweig, Rabbi Yochanan

There seems to be a conflict in Pirkei Avos about how to guard yourself from transgression. One mishna says ‘remind yourself that death is sure to come,' and another mishna says to pay close attention to apply ‘your ear to hear and your eye to see.' Hear how Rabbi Zweig resolves these difference - opening a window for recreating yourself.

Advanced

  
 

How Korach Lost Sight of Himself

 CA 038 B
by Carmell, Rabbi Aryeh

How could Korach so foolishly have publicly challenged Moshe, the nation’s undisputed leader? Rabbi Carmell maps out the path to properly gain distinction. Ironically enough, Korach’s very own sons were the prime examples of choosing the righteous path. Learn how the power of tzitzis and Korach's opening attack fit directly into this concept.

Advanced

  
 

Conflicts With Non-Observant Parents

WY 733

by Weinberg, Rav Yaakov zt"l

How is it possible to respect parents who are hostile toward observant Judaism, and who frown on their child's newfound Torah way of life? Rav Yaakov is both compassionate and clear as he delineates the crucial information that non-observant families lack, and gives guidance on how they should be approached. Learn the parameters of what is non-negotiable, what is possible to give in on, and what to do in cases when you're unsure.
