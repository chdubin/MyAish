Purim with Aishaudio

 

Purim is the ultimate Party Day. This holiday reflects on powerful ideas of our relationship with other nations, living in exile, and God's intense love for the Jewish people. Heighten your celebration with a deeper appreciation of the merriment and hashkafa of this happy day.

Introductory

  
 

Israel After Shalit

 LD 892 A
by Luchins, Dr. David

The decision to free Gilad Shalit from torturous years of captivity and isolation was hotly debated. Besides examining the possible motivations for the Jewish State to finally trade terrorists for the life of their soldier, Dr. Luchins analyzes the social contract Israel has with its citizens, the increasing disgust for Abbas’s dishonest tactics, and the need for a secure, healthy government in Egypt.

Advanced

  
 

Purim: Reaching the True Me

JO 553 H
by Juravel, Mrs. Chana

On Purim, we acquired the Torah more completely than on Sinai, plus we won a tremendous victory against Amalek. How do these great events relate to getting drunk? Follow Mrs. Juravel's unencumbered tale of true spirituality, and the awesome power this day has to save each of us from the state of inner poverty.

Advanced

  
 

Purim: Stripping Evil of its Allure

HT 553 D
by Heller, Rebbitzen Tziporah

The threads that tied Mordechai, Esther, and Haman together from emunah and disbelief. How can we remove the insidious 'doubt' that Amalek poisoned our veins with? Overt and covert tefilllah is what can strip evil of its allure. Rebbitzen Heller gives three essential preparations for Purim, including how to focus on the meaning and to utilize this power of the day in the midst of all the festive preparation.

Advanced

  
 

Ki Sisa: Social Security

WG 021 F
by Wagensberg, Rabbi Abba

There's little doubt that a community can accomplish much more than a lone individual. Moreover, on a mystical level when an individual attaches himself to the whole, he becomes more purified. Rabbi Wagensberg invites you to join up with the Jewish people - and realize the sense of security in not feeling alone.

New Releases

Introductory

  
 

The Rabbi Who Disappeared

 KI 230
by Kahn, Rabbi Ari

Rabbi Feiner takes a close look at the impact song has on the soul. Throughout the centuries Jews have always used the melodies of 'kol (voice of) Yaakov' when they learn Torah and during joyous prayer. Questions in halacha (laws) addressed are: can we use non-Jewish tunes if we change the words; can we listen to non-Jewish music with neutral lyrics; and why a man should not listen to a woman sing who is not closely related to him - and the exceptions to the rule.

Advanced

  
 

Music & Kol Isha

by Feiner, Rabbi Eytan

Throughout the centuries Jews have always used the melodies of 'kol (voice of) Yaakov' when they learn Torah and during joyous prayer. Advanced

  
 

Eishes Chayil: Acceptance vs Change

NY 630 J
by Neustadt, Rebbitzen Yitty

There are things in every spouse that drive their mate crazy. When are we to accept them with a full heart, and when must we beg Hashem for help in changing them? A fascinating class that touches on the whens, whys and hows of criticism, and how to quietly help our husbands grow while showering them with the love and respect they need to succeed.

Advanced

  
 

Mishpatim - Shekalim: Get to the Point

WG 018 G
by Wagensberg, Rabbi Abba

We've all had moments we'd like to forget due to mistakes we've made. Sometimes they can almost make us feel crippled - thinking we are ruined for life. If this resonates, this talk is a must. Rabbi Wagensberg describes a deep place within that is so holy and so beyond the impurities of sin we can always return to, and from where to make a new start.

Introductory

  
 

How Does Fun Relate to Jewish Living?

WY 788
by Weinberg, Rav Yaakov zt"l

In his inimitable way, Rav Yaakov Weinberg analyzes what fun is and why we are so desperate for it. Explained is why so many threads of a today's society are unraveling as a result. Torah cuts far deeper than a coverup for boredom. it fills the soul's yearning for meaning in a way that can be a bit difficult at first - yet the effort is small compared to the payoff.
