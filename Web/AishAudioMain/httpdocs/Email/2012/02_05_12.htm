Weekly Torah Portion: Yisro

New Releases

Introductory

  
 

Jewish Values: Responsibility to Others

 WB 600 D
by Wein, Rabbi Berel

Kindness to others is the hallmark of the Jewish people. Using stories from the Talmud and Tanach, Rabbi Wein walks us through our amazingly packed history of good deeds and charity. From Abraham to Abaye to the present day, hear how rock solid this pillar is to our faith.

Advanced

  
 

The Torah Personality

 KJ 600
by Krohn, Rabbi Paysach

Does simply learning Torah make you a Ben Torah, or is there more to earning this coveted title? Rabbi Krohn answers with excellent tips on using your learning to make you a bigger person. He discusses the importance of having a rebbe, acting responsibly, and making the Torah your business - literally.

Introductory

  
 

A Woman's Take on Orthodox Synagogues

AL 941
by Aiken PhD, Lisa

Probably the hardest thing a secular woman must swallow as she takes her first steps into an Orthodox synagogue is seeing men and women sitting separately. Whether you are newly observant or have struggled with this for many years, listen to Dr. Aiken's refreshing take on the issue. And far from being a bitter pill, hear how this arrangement can be more liberating than it appears.

Advanced

  
 

You Are the Children of Hashem

PS 619 C
by Pincus, R. Shimshon zt"l

By contrasting how humanity is uniquely separate from the massive world of nameless animals, Rabbi Pincus illuminates the extent Hashem loves us as His own children. But the question remains, if we are indeed so close, what forces prevent us from intuitively gravitating to the higher world, and what tools do we need to reach it?

Advanced

  
 

Orchos Tzaddikim: Fearing Hashem - Part One

WN 252 A
by Weinberg, Rabbi Noah zt"l

The fear of God is all-encompassing. Even though one is a scholar, deals well with others, and lives a good life, Heaven will still be critical when this core midah falls short. In this session, Rav Noah discusses the first level of fearing God, which is 'doing the right thing because of society's pressure'. We may think we are above this, but Rav Noah has us laughing at ourselves by revealing the foibles of human thinking.
