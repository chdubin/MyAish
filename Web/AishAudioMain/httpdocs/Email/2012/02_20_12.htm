Weekly Torah Portion: Terumah

New Releases

Introductory

  
 

Rules for Close Relationships

 BA 619 B
by Berger, Rabbi Motty

Rabbi Berger lays out the first important rule of relationships - 'choosing to connect' - and how this leads to true love. Hear a sharp analysis of the parent-child relationship, and how kids need to be moved past this stage as they grow, till the time they decide to really 'choose to give'. How does this rule compare to the give and take existing between us and God, where we have the ability to reject Him or move up to the ultimate relationship.

Advanced

  
 

Developing Peace of Mind 1

 ON 951 E
by Orlowek, Rabbi Noach

In this session, Rabbi Orlowek introduces the concept of peace of mind and how it weaves though so many areas of our lives. What thing must we practice during times of ease so that we can quickly latch onto it when things go frenetic and stressful? Hear the Torah's definition of stress.

Advanced

  
 

Facing Challenges

BB 606 B
by Birnbaum, Rebbitzen Blimi

When big challenges loom before us, it is so hard to muster the internal courage to march onwards and upwards. Rebbetzin Birnbaum gives time-tested encouragement by pointing out a few super painful moments that our nation faced and the underlying greatness that pushed them on. How does the desire to control things warp the picture of the potential growth standing before us?

Advanced

  
 

Classic Lessons in Living

SM 681
by Stern, Rabbi Moshe Aharon zt"l

Whether we tangibly feel it or not, our ancestors made a tremendous impact on our lives by bringing greatness within our reach. Hear how to emulate those whose light we can still feel today, and pass this potential for gadlus (greatness) on to our children and grandchildren. An inspiring talk on the purpose of life.

Introductory

  
 

A Mystical Look at the Wedding Ceremony

SS 613
by Silinsky, Rabbi Shmuel

Grab your dancing shoes and hurry to the chupah for an upbeat 'crash course' on this very holy Jewish event. Travel back with Rabbi Silinsky to the first marriage - Adam and Eve - and then full speed ahead for a peek into the mystical meanings of the marriage ceremony today.
