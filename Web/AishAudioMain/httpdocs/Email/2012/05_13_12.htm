New Releases

Advanced

  
 

When Children Question Our Values

TE 627
by Tauber, Rabbi Ezriel

While it can be unsettling when children question our values, it does show how their minds are alive and concerned. However, what brought them to wonder about truths we hold so dear in the first place? Rabbi Tauber describes what makes your Torah real, what develops a strong desire for spirituality, and how to get your heart in step with your life.

Advanced

  
 

Formal & Informal Prayer

 MN 784
by Miller Karlinsky, Rebbetzin Nechama

Prayer is not primarily reserved for those with moral refinement or spiritual greatness. Rebbenzin Karlinsky explains the benefits of formal and informal prayer, and how your every glance to the Creator expresses true faith in Him. Hear incredible stories and impossible outcomes borne from tefillah, plus excellent answers for 'Why didn't anything change when I prayed with all my heart?'

Introductory

  
 

2 Temples - Miracles & Yearnings

WY 827
by Weinberg, Rav Yaakov zt"l

While it is true that two Temples stood in Jewish history, appreciate they were not at all equal. Rav Weinberg compares the Divine experience and moral character of the nation during the First Temple to the experience during the Second Temple. If this was the difference back then, hear the spiritual gap between those living today to those of the last few generations.

Advanced

  
 

What's In a Name?

 LZ 952
by Leff, Rabbi Zev

We have a life that we are able to maintain, but in truth we've barely scratched its full potential. Rabbi Leff describes how we could be functioning on that higher level, to a point of exercising our emotions for things beyond the immediate present. Were we to perceive the blessings of this elevated state, we would marvel at all the possibilities before us.

Advanced

  
 

Only Vessels Can Receive the Torah

 WN 601 C
by Weinberg, Rabbi Noah zt"l

By illustrating the joy of an uplifting experience Rav Noach connects its attraction and exhilaration to the joy of receiving Torah at Mount Sinai.
